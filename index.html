Overview
What is the Green Asset Exchange
The Green Asset Exchange facilitates the trading of environmental assets such as Carbon Credits and
Renewable Energy Certificates (RECs). In addition to
connecting buyers and sellers, we also provide dedicated
support and services to green-asset project developers,
ensuring their projects thrive and their green-assets reach
a tradable status.

Listed green assets

SA Tax Carbon Credits
South African Carbon Credits are a type of
environmental commodity used to represent a
reduction or removal of one metric ton of Carbon
Dioxide emissions. 5-10% of the tax liability from a
company's carbon-emmissions can be covered by
South African Tax compliant Carbon Credits.
Renewable Energy Certificates
Renewable Energy Certificates (RECS) are tradable
certificates that represent the environmental and
renewable attributes associated with one
megawatt-hour (MWh) of electricity generated
from renewable sources. RECs provide a way to
track and verify the production and use of
renewable energy.
Global Voluntary Carbon
Credits
Voluntary Carbon Credits are a type of
environmental commodity used to represent a
reduction or removal of one metric ton of Carbon
Dioxide emissions. Voluntary carbon credits are
purchased by individuals, companies and
organisations for voluntary use, rather than to
comply with legally binding emissions reduction
obligations.

A Green Asset is an asset that is invested in or directly
contributes to environmentally friendly and sustainable
initiatives.
These assets are designed to have a positive impact on the Environment, either by reducing emmissions, conserving resources, promoting renewable energy or protecting
